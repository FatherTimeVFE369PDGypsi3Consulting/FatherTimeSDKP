The SharonCare1 propulsion architecture begins not with engineering convention but with geometry. The SD&N (Shape × Dimension × Number) Principle of the FatherTimeSDKP framework establishes that every geometric form encodes its own higher-dimensional states through three intrinsic properties: Faces (F), Vertices (V), and Edges (E). The design of SharonCare1 is the physical instantiation of this principle applied to spacecraft propulsion.

The selection of 12 through-body plasma conduits is not arbitrary. It is the direct application of the dodecahedron — the Platonic solid with F=12 faces — as the governing geometry of the propulsion system. The spherical hull of SharonCare1 is geometrically a dodecahedron, and every structural and propulsive element of the design follows from that single geometric fact.

1.1  Why the Dodecahedron

The dodecahedron is the Platonic solid with the highest number of faces before the geometry becomes impractical for physical conduit routing (the icosahedron has 20 faces, which produces too-shallow intersection angles at the plenum). With 12 faces, the dodecahedral arrangement provides:

  • Maximum angular distribution — face centers separated by approximately 41.8°, ensuring no two conduits compete for the same thrust vector
  • Structural completeness — 30 edge pathways form the full internal skeletal framework of the hull
  • 4,096 discrete thrust states — 212 binary gate combinations provide continuous 360° vectoring on all axes
  • Dual-solid stability — shared E=30 with the icosahedron provides the 5D encoding that makes the geometry dimensionally resistant to deformation under plasma pressure

2System Architecture

2.1  Central Distribution Manifold

The propulsion system abandons the conventional architecture of external gimbaled engines in favor of a continuous, unified plasma circuit integrated directly into the structural hull. Twelve through-body plasma conduits run the full diameter of the spherical hull, intersecting at the geometric center to form the Central Mixing Chamber — the Plenum.

2.2  The Plenum — Central Mixing Chamber

All twelve conduits converge at the geometric center of the spherical hull. This junction serves as the primary distribution hub and self-regulating flow equalizer. By forcing all plasma to transit through this single central chamber, the system ensures pressure equalization and thermal uniformity across the full conduit array before injection into the thrust vectors.

Kinetic turbulence encountered by any single conduit is instantly dampened and redistributed across the remaining manifold via the plenum's pressure-equalization dynamics. This prevents localized hull stress and maintains stable flight characteristics without active intervention from the flight computer — the geometry handles it passively.

3Magnetic Flux Gating — Vector Control System

Directional authority is achieved through Magnetic Flux Gating at the exit ports of each conduit. This system replaces all conventional mechanical valves, gimbals, and thrust-vectoring actuators with high-fidelity electromagnetic arrays. There are no moving mechanical parts in the propulsion vector control chain.

3.1  Gate Architecture — 20-Node Control Lattice

The SD&N encoding places 20 vertex nodes (V=20) at the dodecahedral vertices of the hull. These 20 nodes are the physical locations of the magnetic flux gate arrays — each controlling the plasma flow at the exit ports of adjacent conduits. The V=20 configuration creates a fully connected 4D control state: every gate is within one vertex-edge step of every conduit exit port.

3.2  Thrust Vector Mathematics

The flight computer does not calculate physical orientation angles. It calculates the Thrust Vector Summation — the net force vector produced by a given gate configuration across all 12 conduits. For any desired velocity change vector ΔV = (x, y, z), the Dynamic Distribution Algorithm solves:

ΔV_net = Σ(i=1 to 12) [g_i · T_i · n_i]

4Propellant Feed and Storage Architecture

4.1  Conformal Toroidal Tank Configuration

Propellant is housed in conformal, toroidal-shaped tanks mounted along the inner radius of the spherical hull's secondary layer. The toroidal geometry is selected specifically for its compatibility with the dodecahedral structural frame: each of the 30 SD&N edge pathways serves as a structural stringer between adjacent toroidal tank segments.

This positioning ensures the center of mass remains constant regardless of propellant depletion level — the toroidal ring empties uniformly from all sectors simultaneously, maintaining the balanced mass distribution required for the gate-modulated attitude control system.

4.2  Cryogenic Injection and Regenerative Cooling

  • High-pressure cryogenic pump system feeds directly into the central Plenum. The pump system provides variable mass-flow rates: low-flow for station-keeping and high-flow for orbital maneuvering and transfer burns
  • Regenerative cooling loop: propellant circulates around the outer jacket of each conduit before injection, absorbing heat from the conduit wall and pre-heating the fuel. This simultaneously protects the conduit structure and increases thermodynamic efficiency by delivering pre-heated propellant to the Plenum
  • Plenum pre-conditioning: propellant is thermally and atomically pre-conditioned in the central Plenum before entering the conduit array, ensuring uniform plasma state across all 12 thrust vectors at ignition

6Avionics, Control System and Telemetry

6.1  Fiber-Optic Command Bus Architecture

The Plenum connects to all 12 magnetic flux gate arrays via a radiation-hardened, fiber-optic command bus. The fiber-optic medium is selected specifically to eliminate electromagnetic interference (EMI) from the plasma flow — copper bus architectures are inherently susceptible to EMI-induced command errors in high-plasma environments.

6.2  Dynamic Distribution Algorithm

The flight computer implements the Dynamic Distribution Algorithm — a real-time solver that converts incoming navigation commands into gate configuration matrices. The algorithm operates on the SD&N vertex graph of the dodecahedral geometry: each gate node (V=20) is addressed as a node in a 4D control graph, and the solver finds the minimum-energy gate configuration that produces the commanded thrust vector within the angular resolution of 5.81°.

Failover within the algorithm: if a gate reports a fault, the algorithm detects the pressure drop signature within one control cycle (<1 ms) and automatically re-solves the gate configuration matrix using the remaining 11 conduits to maintain the commanded thrust vector with graceful degradation.

7.2  Conduit-as-Chassis Integration

All avionics, life support, and payload components are mounted directly to the internal conduit structures, which serve as the primary load-bearing skeleton of the craft. This conduit-as-chassis design eliminates the need for a separate internal frame, maximizing usable volume. Equipment mounting points utilize active piezoelectric dampers to neutralize micro-vibrations generated by plasma flow.

8Redundancy and Fail-Safe Protocols

8.1  Graceful Degradation — N+1 Architecture

The central manifold design provides inherent N+1 redundancy without additional hardware. If any single conduit suffers a blockage, gate failure, or structural compromise, the central Plenum automatically re-routes plasma to the remaining 11 conduits. The Dynamic Distribution Algorithm detects the pressure drop signature within <1 ms and re-solves the thrust vector matrix for the 11-conduit configuration.

REDUNDANCY MATRIX — CONDUIT FAILURE SCENARIOS
12/12 conduits:  Full thrust authority — 4,096 discrete states — all operations nominal
11/12 conduits:  Graceful degradation — 2,048 discrete states — full mission capability
10/12 conduits:  Reduced vectoring — 1,024 states — primary mission with constraints
 9/12 conduits:  Minimum viable — 512 states — emergency return/station-keeping only
<9/12 conduits:  Emergency protocol — passive attitude control — emergency beacon active

Fail-closed design: power loss → maximum magnetic pinch on ALL gates → full shutdown
No conduit failure can produce an uncontrolled thrust event.

8.2  Emergency Protocols

  • Fail-closed emergency shutdown: All magnetic flux gates are designed as fail-closed. If primary power is lost, the magnetic field defaults to maximum pinch state on all 12 exit ports. Plasma flow stops within microseconds. No mechanical actuator failure mode can produce uncontrolled thrust
  • Structural safety factor: Spherical hull rated for 1.5× maximum operating Plenum pressure. Each conduit is double-walled with vacuum gap between the primary plasma bore and the external hull wall
  • Emergency Plenum venting: Passive secondary venting system depressurizes the central Plenum into deep space within 10 seconds if internal pressure exceeds safe operating limits
  • Thermal runaway protection: Redundant thermocouple arrays in each conduit wall segment report to the flight computer at 1 kHz. Automatic gate closure initiated if conduit wall temperature approaches W-Re safety threshold
